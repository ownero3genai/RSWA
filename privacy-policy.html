Privacy Policy

How we handle and protect your personal data.

Last Updated: March 2024

At Realtors Social Welfare Association (RSWA), we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your data when you visit our website or become a member of our association.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us through forms, registrations, and communication. This may include:

  • Personal Details: Name, email address, phone number, and physical address.
  • Professional Information: RERA registration number, company name, designation, and branch affiliation.
  • Communication Data: Messages, inquiries, or feedback sent through our contact forms or support channels.

We may also automatically collect certain information when you navigate our site, such as IP addresses, browser types, and usage data via cookies to enhance user experience.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is strictly used to provide, maintain, and improve our services to you as a member or visitor. Specifically, we use it to:

  • Process your membership application and maintain your member profile.
  • Communicate with you regarding association updates, events, legal changes, and administrative notices.
  • Provide customer support and respond to your inquiries.
  • Monitor and analyze website usage and trends to improve our digital platform.

3. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal identification information to others. We may share generic aggregated demographic information not linked to any personal identification information with our business partners and trusted affiliates for the purposes outlined above.

We may disclose your personal information only if required to do so by law or in the good faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect and defend the rights or property of RSWA, or protect the personal safety of members or the public.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the personal data we collect and process about you. The measures we use are designed to provide a level of security appropriate to the risk of processing your personal information.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location and applicable data protection laws, you may have the right to access, correct, update, or request deletion of your personal information. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided below.

6. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

7. Contact Us
