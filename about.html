Our Origin

The RSWA Story

In 2010, a group of 18 dedicated real estate professionals from Andheri came together with a single shared vision: to create a platform where channel partners, builders, and consultants could work with dignity, legal protection, and mutual support.

What began as a small informal collective rapidly grew into a formal registered association as more professionals recognized the need for a unified voice in dealings with developers, regulators, and financial institutions.

Today, RSWA represents over 350 members across 4 branches spanning the entire Western rail corridor — from Mumbai to Boisar — and continues to be the region's most respected real estate welfare body.

Registered Association Formally registered under the Societies Registration Act with full legal recognition.
RERA Cell Since 2018 Dedicated RERA compliance cell established to protect members after the Act's implementation.
Democratic Governance Elected leadership renewed every two years through transparent democratic elections.

Purpose & Direction

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

Empowering Every Realtor

To be the unwavering support system for real estate professionals across the Western corridor — protecting their rights, enhancing their capabilities, and fostering an ethical, transparent, and cooperative industry environment where every member can achieve their professional and personal goals.

Our Vision

A Thriving Industry Ecosystem

To build a fully organized, professional, and self-regulating real estate industry in the Mumbai–Boisar corridor where channel partners and consultants are valued, legally protected, and recognized as essential contributors to India's housing mission and economic development.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

These principles guide every decision we make and every service we provide to our members.

Integrity

We uphold honesty and transparency in all dealings — with members, developers, regulators, and the public. Trust is the foundation of everything we do.

Solidarity

We believe in the power of collective action. Every member's strength contributes to the whole, and we stand together in protecting the interests of our community.

Excellence

We continuously raise the bar through professional development, training programs, and adoption of best practices that keep our members ahead of the curve.

Fairness

We ensure equitable treatment for all members regardless of seniority, geography, or background. Our mediation processes are impartial and principled.

Growth

We are committed to the professional and personal growth of every member through resources, mentorship, and opportunities that expand their capabilities.

Welfare

Beyond professional support, we care deeply about the well-being of our members and their families — providing social welfare programs and community assistance.

Our Journey

Milestones That Shaped Us

From a small group of 18 professionals to a 350+ member network — every step of the RSWA journey.

2010

RSWA Founded

18 real estate professionals from Andheri come together to form the Realtors Social Welfare Association with a founding charter focused on member welfare and ethical practice.

2012

First Branch — Virar

Growing demand from the Western corridor leads to the opening of the first branch office in Virar West, bringing 40+ new members into the fold.

2015

500 Member Milestone

RSWA crosses 500 registered members — a landmark achievement celebrated with a grand annual summit attended by prominent real estate developers and government officials.

2018

RERA Cell Established

Following the Real Estate Regulation Act's implementation, RSWA establishes a dedicated RERA Compliance Cell staffed by legal experts to help members navigate the new regulatory landscape.

2020

Boisar Branch Opens

Recognizing Boisar's rapid industrial and residential development, RSWA opens its fourth branch in the MIDC area, extending the association's reach to the far northern corridor.

2024

Present Day

RSWA today serves 350+ active members across 4 branches, having facilitated over ₹500 Crores in property transactions and served 10,000+ families across the Western corridor.
